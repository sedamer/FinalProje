What is Body Mass Index (BMI)?

The calculation method that shows the estimated fat percentage in an individual's body is called body mass index (BMI) or height-weight index. When calculating BMI, the person's height and weight are taken as basis. By looking at the result obtained, the closeness or distance of the person's current weight to his ideal weight is observed.

Nowadays, especially people and athletes trying to gain or lose weight try to pay attention to their BMI ratios. That's why it's so important to understand that there is no single ideal weight for everyone. This is a type of parameter used to determine whether a person's body weight is normal for their height. The ideal weight for people of different height, gender and age varies depending on these factors.


How to Calculate Body Mass Index?

When calculating body mass index, the individual's weight and height are taken as basis. Therefore, first of all, height and weight must be calculated accurately. The calculation is made in the same way for individuals from all age groups. However, it should not be forgotten that each age group is classified differently. Children, teenagers, adults and the elderly are evaluated within their own age groups. When calculating BMI, body mass is divided by the square of height. Subsequently, the units kg/m² obtained from the mass in kg and height in meters are used for calculation.

If an example calculation is given, the calculation is done as follows:

  • Weight: 60kg
  • Height: 1.70 m
  • BMI: 60 / (1.70)*(1.70) = 20.8 kg/m²

What Do Body Mass Index Results Mean?

The result obtained in BMI calculation is not related to the fat percentage in your body, body type, fat and muscle tissue. Therefore, BMI alone should not be taken into account when making an evaluation. Based only on BMI and ignoring other factors would be ignoring many factors related to a person's health.

In addition to waist measurement, metabolic rate measurement and detailed body analysis also enable a more detailed analysis of the individual's weight problem. Metabolic rate measurement is done by wearing a mask connected to a special device for 15 minutes. The person breathes normally while wearing the mask. Metabolic rate is measured by calculating the individual's average oxygen consumption, breathing amount, breathing frequency and average oxygen density. All these factors should be taken into consideration when evaluating a person's weight. Whether the individual is below or above his ideal weight should be evaluated by a physician, taking into account all these factors. You should also work with a specialist physician to improve existing weight-related health problems or prevent future health problems.

Evaluation of BMI Results

  • For results below 18.5 kg/m²: Under ideal weight
  • For results between 18.5 kg/m² and 24.9 kg/m²: At ideal weight
  • For results between 25 kg/m² and 29.9 kg/m²: Above ideal weight
  • For results between 30 kg/m² and 39.9 kg/m²: Extremely over ideal weight (Obese)
